Meet The Board

Ralston Alexander Board Member

Ralston has been in the private practive of law in Victoria for a long time - currently as a partner with Cook Roberts LLP. His preferred practice areas include institutional representations such as School Districts, Health Authorities, Government and the Land Title and Survey Authority.

He has served on many volunteer boards over the years with a concentration on the United Way. He was the President of the Law Society of BC in 2005.

Lisa Banks Advisor

Lisa is a native Victorian with a degree in Nursing (UBC) and a Master's in Exercise Physiology (U. of Tulsa). She spent a few years working at VGH and then a few as a Public Health Nurse in the early 90's, favoring prevention to acute care. She has always been interested in Health Promotion, and currently works for Saanich as the Health Services Supervisor.

Her interest in exercise paralleled her career, and during her time in University and the years following, she becamse a Strength and Conditioning specialist for the University of Tulsa and University of Nebraska, an Olympic Weightlifting Coach (first female coach in Canada), and exercise therapist to the national swim and triathlon teams. She has done facility design for several Health and Fitness centres including UBC, EA sports in Vancouver, Gorge Fitness, and the 2007 expansion of Saanich Commonwealth Place. Along with Sandy Gilmour she is on the of the founding members of the Take Heart Community Based Cardiac Rehabilitation Program. Her main interest in the project is to see Victoria's community better served in the area of Chronic disease management.

Randy Decksheimer Board Member

Randy is the office managing partner in KPMG's Victoria office. He has over 30 years of experience primarily overseeing audits and the provision of tax and advisory services to various organizations including public sector entities, and both private and publicly traded companies. He is familiar with financial requirements in operating in the public sector including those related to a number of clients in the health sector including VIHA, private care facilities and medical practices. Randy brings an expectation to commit resources from KPMG to the 'wellness' project as needed.

Randy also has considerable experience in providing services to and volunteering with a number of not for profit organisations.

Allan Gilchrist Board Member

Allan was born in Manitoba and received his BScEE from the University of Manitoba. His background spans a career in international marketing to the foundation of Nokia Products Ltd. His 25 years as President and CEO of Nokia Canada earned him a Knighthood - The Order of the Lion, First Class - from the government of Finland. He presided over Nokia's sponsorship of Canada'a men's curling championship the Nokia Brier from 2001 through to 2004.

Allan has sat on the board of the Finnish Chamber of Commerce, The Victoria Hospital Foundation, and the Sandra Schmirler Foundation. As a literacy advocate, he has worked with the Peter Gzowski Foundation both through Nokia and on an individual basis. Allan sat as Chair of the Board of Advisors for the Peter B. Gustavson School of Business at the Univeristy of Victoria and continues his involvement with the business school through its mentorship program.

Eileen Gilchrist Board Member

Born in Manitoba, Eileen retired from a 25 year career in the international aerospace world where she served as corportate secretary and executive assistant to the president's office. Dowty Aeropsace Ltd. founded by Sir George Dowty in England during WWII later joined forces with Messier-Bugatti of France and now has offices around the world. Prior positions include assistant to the Dean of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Waterloo and a certificate of general insurance from Queens University with 9 years in the industry.

Interested in community, Eileen has been involved in the Peter Gzowski Foundation for literacy for over 25 years both in Ontario and in Victoria and has offered her services to the University of Victoria business school and the Sandra Schmirler Foundation.

Sandy Gilmour Advisor

Sandy Gilmour is originally from Winnipeg and moved to the west coast in 1986. She did her Kinesiology degree at University of Waterloo and is also a Registered Nurse and an ACSM Exercise Specialist. She has specialised in orthopaedics, emergency and cardiology and has over 26 years of experience in cardiac rehab. She has experience working in many different cardiac and chronic disease rehab program models including the RehFit Center in Winnipeg. Sandy currently works as Case Manager in the Cardiac Rehab Department at Royal Jubilee Hospital and as a Program Deliverer/Instructor in the Take Heart Community Cardiac Rehab Program at the Saanich Commonwealth Place. She loves to teach and has developed and taught numerous cardiac rehab courses for staff as well as clients/patients. Sandy currently teaches cardiac and chronic disease rehab at Camosun College, BCIT, and in the UBC Physiotherapy program.

Sandy is very enthused about developing a more comprehensive cardiac and chronic disease rehab program and facility for the Victoria area. She was involved on the ground floor with the development for the Take Heart Program here in Victoria that started in 2002. She is keen to see that patients in have access to a rehab program that is accessible, affordable, available and provides continued ongoing assistance to promote their better health.

Sandy also love to kayak, work with puppets and particpate in storytelling.

John Martin Board Member

Born and raised in Toronto (Port Credit), John is a graduate of the HBA programme at UWO's Business School with a minor in Economics. John spent 32 years in the life insurance industry with focus on sales and marketing - Canada Life; Sun Life and North American Life. He came to Victoria to be President of a firm which was owned by 10 credit unions that enabled them to provide a financial planning service to their members. John dealt with 33 credit unions and supported over 300 financial planners. He served as President of a subsidiary of Coast Capital Savings that held a unique cross marketing platform involving: general insurance; life insurance; mutual and segregated funds; mortgage brokering and deposit brokering.

John has served on the board of Advocis the association for life insurers across Canada and headed a subsidiary to develop and offer a captive Public Liability Programme for Life Insurance Agents and Financial Planners. He is also the former Vice-President of Community Living Victoria Foundation and currently is chair of Uplands Golf Club's annual Heart Tournament. He also is proud to be a more than satisfied "graduate" of the Caridac Rehabilitation Programme.

John is an avid skier, golfer and bridge fanatic.

Gordon Plewes Board Member

Gordon was raised and educated in Edmonton, Alberta and the University of Alberta, and practiced law in that City for 36 years, ending his practice as a partner in the national law firm now known as Denton’s Canada LLP. He retired to Victoria in 2010. His practice consisted of all areas of corporate/ commercial law, including business consultation and advisory matters, business organization and taxation, and the buying and selling of business enterprises.

Gordon has had the opportunity of serving on several boards of various non-profit organizations in social, community, health and public areas. Particularly, he was active with the redevelopment of the Art Gallery of Alberta and chairman of the Edmonton Chamber of Commerce and World Trade Center Edmonton. He also served as the president of the Alberta and Canadian Lung Associations. Gordon is currently the treasurer of the Rotary Club of Victoria and the secretary of the Uplands Golf Club.

Dr Elizabeth Swiggum Advocate

Dr Elizabeth Swiggum has worked at the Royal Jubilee Hospital since 2005 as a Cardiologist and Medical Director of the Cardiac Rehabilitation program and Heart Function clinic. Prior to her arrival, sick Heart Failure patients who required advice and management of their chronic condition were sometimes referred to the Heart Function clinic in Vancouver. Patients who were "too high" risk to exercise without physician supervision were not supported in our community. The Cardiac Rehabilitation program now offers multidisciplinary care for secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease.

Over the past 8 years there has been a dedicated effort to reach patients throughtout Vancouver Island including heart function clinics in Nanaimo and Campbell River and Cardiac Rehabilitation Community "Take Heart" programs throughout the island. She is dedicated to teaching residents and currently holds a clinical assistant appointment with UBC and affiliated appointment at University of Victoria.

Howard Waldner Board Member

Howard Waldner is the past President and Chief Executive Officer of Vancouver Island Health Authority, serving in this role from 2004 until 2013. He is curently CEO of Caledonia Solutions, a strategic consulting company he founded in 2013. He is a visionary and innovative leader with over 30 years of experience of managing health sysytems in both Canada and the U.K. He has a keen interest in system innovation, transformation and performance improvement. He is also passionate in the area of health facility planning, design and procurement and has led the development of number of large and successful hospital projects using both traditional and alternate funding approaches. He received the Canadian College of Health Leaders National Award for Innovation in Health in 2013.

He is a certified member of the Institute of Corporate Directors, and currently serves as an Independent Board Director of Strata-Health Solutions, a successful Calgary based Health IT company, and is a partner with Prioritize Software, a Vancouver based strategic solutions company. He also serves as a volunteer member of the Telus (Victoria) Community Board and has served on a number of “non-profit” Boards, including the Canadian Institute for Health Information, the BC Academic Health Council, (which he chaired for 5 years), the United Way Campaign Cabinet, Carewest, VRRI, and the Greater Victoria Coalition to end Homelessness. Prior to joining VIHA in 2004, he was the Deputy Chief Executive and Executive Vice President and Chief Operating Officer for the former Calgary Health Region from 1999 until 2004. Before moving to Calgary in 1999, he held a number of senior leadership roles within the Scottish National Health Service, most recently as the Chief Executive of Dundee University Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust.

He received his Executive MBA from the University of Glasgow, Scotland, and is a Certified member of the Canadian College of Health Leaders and a Fellow of the United Kingdom Institute of Health Care Managers. He is Adjunct Professor in the Faculty of Medicine of the University of British Columbia, and teaches on a number of executive leadership and masters programs in the School of Public and Population Health.

About BeFit

We want our community to have the opportunity to choose and live a health aging lifestyle.
